Implementation of the Quantitative Analysis Technique to evaluate cloud-based Enterprise Architectures through the Archi tool

Franco Gil 1

 1  University of Carabobo | Experimental Faculty of Science and Technology
| Bsc. Thesis | Code |
   

Abstract

The observability of a technological product is a necessary element during the software development life cycle. Few projects add observability as an activity when solving a business problem or in the delivery of products to their customers, generating insecurities such as the result of exposing the service to users, the prediction of its performance, the monitoring of possible services that have been developed with low quality, etc., putting at risk the effort and time invested by the technological collaborators and the organization's leaders. Enterprise Architectures (EA) expose a coherent set of methods and models that can be used in the design and implementation of the organizational structure in a company. Quantitative Analysis is a good element of study to answer the uncertainties that motivate towards the observability of a technology product. In the present research, a Quantitative Analysis technique is used on EA that influences the observability of this one thanks to the use of cloud computing services that offer a set of quantitative data and resource utilization metrics useful as input data for algorithms and formal ideas that expose workload and performance values along the EA and its components. The result of this research is the implementation of a Quantitative Analysis technique to evaluate EA based on cloud services through the Archi tool together with its jArchi plug-in, allowing to contrast different simulations of the whole architecture under real workloads and utilization environments.
